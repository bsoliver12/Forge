⚕ Retatrutide — 3-Meal Protocol GLP-1/GIP/GCG Tri-Agonist

Retatrutide is a triple agonist (GLP-1, GIP, glucagon receptor) — significantly more potent than semaglutide or tirzepatide for appetite suppression and fat loss. Three meals per day is appropriate and realistic on this compound. The appetite suppression creates a natural caloric deficit on top of the keto structure. Key considerations: eat slowly (GLP-1 slows gastric emptying — overeating causes significant nausea), prioritize protein density at every meal since volume is limited, and never skip the pre-bed collagen/protein — muscle preservation is critical given the strong appetite suppression.

  • Meal spacing: ~5–6 hours between meals. Roughly 8 AM → 1–2 PM → 7 PM works well on training days.
  • If nauseated: Reduce meal volume. Split the protein shake into two portions if needed. Never force food through nausea — listen to the drug.
  • Protein priority: If you can only finish part of a meal, prioritize protein over fat. Fat can be made up elsewhere; protein loss on retatrutide accelerates muscle wasting.
